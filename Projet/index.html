What is spotify ?

Spotify is an audio streaming platform launched by Spotify AB on 7 October 2008.
It provides access to over 40 millions DRM-protected tracks and podcasts from record labels and media companies.
It follows a freemium policy (basic features are available free of charge with advertisement while additional features are locked behind a subscription paywall) ;
this has allowed the website to gather 207 million monthly active users, including 96 million paying subscribers (as of February 2019)." [1]

As you can see below, it is popular in multiple countries in both Europe, America and Asia.
Recently, a dataset of the stream count of the top 200 songs of Spotify per day in 2017 was released. That dataset can be used to answer some questions, such as :
which songs were popular in each country last year? The redder the country, the more Spotify is popular. Countries in black do not have access to Spotify.

What's in a song ?

  • Danceability describes how suitable a track is for dancing.
  • Energy represents a perceptual measure of intensity and activity.
  • Instrumentalness is a rating of whether the track has vocals.
  • Acousticness is a rating of whether the track is vocal.
  • Liveness is a rating of whether the track is performed live.
  • Valence describes the musical positiveness conveyed by a track.
  • Loudness is the overall loudness of a track in decibels (dB).
  • Speechiness is a rating of how much of the track has vocals.
  • Tempo is the estimated tempo of a track. [2]
