What is spotify ?

Spotify is an audio streaming platform launched by Spotify AB on 7 October 2008. It provides access to over 40 millions DRM-protected tracks and podcasts from record labels and media companies. It follows a freemium policy (basic features are available free of charge with advertisement while additional features are locked behind a subscription paywall) ; this has allowed the website to gather 207 million monthly active users, including 96 million paying subscribers (as of February 2019)." [1]
As you can see on the right, it is popular in multiple countries in both Europe and America. Recently, a dataset of the stream count of the top 200 songs of Spotify per day was released. That dataset can be used to answer some questions, such as : which songs are popular in which country, and when ?

What's in a song ?

  • Danceability describes how suitable a track is for dancing.
  • Energy represents a perceptual measure of intensity and activity.
  • Instrumentalness is a rating of whether the track has vocals.
  • Acousticness is a rating of whether the track is vocal.
  • Liveness is a rating of whether the track is performed live.
  • Valence describes the musical positiveness conveyed by a track.
  • Loudness is the overall loudness of a track in decibels (dB).
  • Speechiness is a rating of how much of the track has vocals.
  • Tempo estimated tempo of a track. [2]
